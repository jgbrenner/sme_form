Subject Matter Expert Red-Team Evaluation of the KARL-30

You are participating in an adversarial validation exercise to stress-test the KARL-30, an ultra-short distillation of the 300-item IPIP-NEO (Goldberg, 1999) Big Five inventory.

Context & Mission: This scale operates at the absolute limit of the bandwidth–fidelity tradeoff: exactly one item per facet. Each item is a single load-bearing pillar—a structural failure does not merely degrade reliability; it severs our ability to measure that facet.

Target Deployment: Adult WEIRD samples (Western, Educated, Industrialized, Rich, and Democratic) typical of online administration.

Rules of Engagement: Evaluate each item exactly as written. Do not attempt to rewrite or fix the phrasing. The audit follows a two-stage threshold:

1. Vulnerability Check

  • Pass: The item isolates the target facet cleanly enough to serve as its sole indicator.
  • Flag: The item exhibits a specific structural vulnerability from our vector taxonomy.

2. Disposition (If Flagged)

  • Acceptable Risk: A vulnerability exists, but the item is still usable as the sole marker.
  • Fatal Risk: The vulnerability is catastrophic; the item cannot survive as the sole marker and must be replaced.
Failure Vectors:

(You will select these via on-screen buttons; you do not need to memorize them.)

  • Proxy: Measures a demographic/cultural proxy (e.g., wealth, education, ideology) instead of the trait.
  • State: Driven by temporary mood, fatigue, or immediate environment rather than a stable trait.
  • Ambiguity: Lexically or contextually vague, enabling multiple valid interpretations.
  • Fakability: Highly transparent and easily gamed for social desirability.
  • Drift: Discriminant collapse; the item inadvertently measures a completely different trait.
  • Other: Use only if none of the above fit, and specify the mechanism.

System Constraints & Consent:

  • Autosave: Your progress is saved locally on every interaction. You may close this window and return later.
  • Pacing: This exercise is not timed. It should take ~5–15 minutes, but take as long as necessary.
  • Privacy: Please do not include Personally Identifiable Information in the comments.
  • Consent: Participation is fully voluntary. You may abandon this exercise at any point.

Respondent Cognitive Environment (Johnson, 2014)

To evaluate an item's vulnerability, you must evaluate it under the exact constraints placed on the test-taker. Below are the original instructions presented to respondents.

"The following pages contain phrases describing people's behaviors. Please use the rating scale next to each phrase to describe how accurately each statement describes you.

Describe yourself as you generally are now, not as you wish to be in the future. Describe yourself as you honestly see yourself, in relation to other people you know of the same sex as you are, and roughly your same age.

So that you can describe yourself in an honest manner, your responses will be kept in absolute confidence."
